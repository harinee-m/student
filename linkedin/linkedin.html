Connect with Peers

  • Search for your university's official LinkedIn page and join its alumni network
  • Connect with classmates from your major and student organizations
  • Follow professors and teaching assistants from your department
  • Personalize connection requests with a brief note about how you know them
  • Join university-affiliated groups and participate in discussions

Connect with Industry Professionals

  • Follow companies you're interested in working for
  • Connect with recruiters from target companies (include a note about your interest)
  • Find alumni from your school working at companies you admire
  • Engage with their content by liking and commenting thoughtfully
  • Attend virtual events hosted by companies and connect with speakers

Content Engagement Strategy

  • Comment on industry posts with insightful perspectives
  • Share articles relevant to your field with your own commentary
  • Post updates about your learning journey and projects
  • Celebrate milestones like course completions or project launches
  • Use relevant hashtags like #ComputerScience #Programming #TechStudent

LinkedIn Profile Checklist

LinkedIn's official guide to creating an all-star profile:

  • Profile photo that shows your face clearly
  • Headline that goes beyond your job title
  • Detailed experience with accomplishments
  • Education history
  • Skills with at least 3 endorsements each
  • At least 50 connections

Translating Your Resume to LinkedIn

Key differences between resumes and LinkedIn profiles:

  • LinkedIn allows for more narrative in the About section
  • You can include all experiences, not just most recent/relevant
  • Skills can be more extensive and get validated through endorsements
  • Projects can have their own dedicated sections
  • You can include media links and samples of your work
